## Monitoring Networks & Research Initiatives

### Permafrost Pathways (Woodwell Climate Research Center)

**Description:** Arctic carbon monitoring network combining eddy-covariance flux towers with soil carbon measurements.

**Resources:**
- **Website:** https://www.woodwellclimate.org/project/permafrost-pathways/
- **Focus:** Integrated monitoring of permafrost carbon fluxes (CO₂ and CH₄)
- **Key Features:**
  - Eddy-covariance flux network across Arctic sites
  - Ground-based carbon stock measurements
  - Permafrost thaw monitoring
  - MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, Verification) framework development
  - Modeling and forecasting tools

**Relevance for This Workflow:**
- **Field protocols:** Standardized methods for soil carbon sampling in permafrost
- **MRV framework:** Guidance for temporal monitoring and verification
- **Monitoring gaps:** Identifies priority areas for expanded monitoring
- **Data access:** Potential for accessing flux tower data for validation

**Citation:**
- Permafrost Pathways Project. Woodwell Climate Research Center. https://www.woodwellclimate.org/project/permafrost-pathways/

---

### PermafrostNet (NSERC Permafrost Partnership Network)

**Description:** Canadian research network focused on permafrost monitoring, adaptation, and community engagement.

**Resources:**
- **Website:** https://permafrostnet.ca/
- **Focus:** Permafrost science, monitoring, and adaptation for Canadian communities
- **Key Features:**
  - Pan-Canadian permafrost research coordination
  - Community-based monitoring integration
  - Infrastructure risk assessment
  - Knowledge mobilization and training
  - Multi-disciplinary approach (engineering, ecology, social science)

**Relevance for This Workflow:**
- **Monitoring sites:** Access to established permafrost monitoring locations
- **Protocols:** Standardized Canadian methods for permafrost measurement
- **Collaboration:** Partnership opportunities for field campaigns
- **Regional expertise:** Local knowledge for site selection

**Citation:**
- PermafrostNet. NSERC Permafrost Partnership Network. https://permafrostnet.ca/

### Circumpolar Active Layer Monitoring (CALM) Network

**Description:** International network of standardized active layer monitoring sites, including extensive Canadian coverage.

**Resources:**
- **Website:** https://www2.gwu.edu/~calm/
- **Canadian Sites:** https://permafrost.gi.alaska.edu/sites_map
- **Focus:** Long-term active layer thickness (ALT) monitoring
- **Key Features:**
  - Standardized CALM grid protocol (100m × 100m grids)
  - Mechanical frost probing
  - Multi-decadal time series (1990s-present)
  - Spatial variability characterization
  - Open data access

**Relevance for This Workflow:**
- **Active layer depth data:** Historical and current ALT measurements for validation
- **Site co-location:** Potential to co-locate carbon sampling with CALM sites
- **Temporal trends:** Long-term active layer deepening rates
- **Protocols:** Standardized frost probe methodology

**Data Access:**
- **GTN-P Database:** Global Terrestrial Network for Permafrost
- **Contact:** CALM coordinators for site-specific data

**Citation:**
- Brown, J., et al. (2000). Circumpolar Active Layer Monitoring (CALM) Program Network. *Polar Geography*, 24(3), 166-176.

## Soil and Carbon Databases

### Northern Terrestrial Ecosystem Database (NTED)

**Description:** Comprehensive database of Arctic and boreal ecosystem properties, including soil carbon.

**Resources:**
- **Database:** Maintained by Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL DAAC)
- **URL:** https://daac.ornl.gov/cgi-bin/dsviewer.pl?ds_id=1237
- **Focus:** Vegetation, soil, and carbon data for northern ecosystems
- **Key Variables:**
  - Soil organic carbon (SOC) stocks
  - Bulk density
  - Soil texture
  - Vegetation type
  - Active layer depth (select sites)
  - Geographic coordinates

**Coverage:**
- Pan-Arctic (includes Canadian Arctic)
- ~500+ sites with soil carbon data

**Relevance for This Workflow:**
- **Literature values:** SOC concentration and bulk density by ecosystem type
- **Scenario modeling:** Reference carbon stocks for intact vs. degraded permafrost
- **Validation:** Independent data for model validation
- **Site selection:** Identify data-rich regions

**Data Access:**
- Free download from ORNL DAAC
- Requires citation

**Citation:**
- Bockheim, J.G., et al. (2015). The Northern Terrestrial Ecosystem Database (NTED). *ORNL DAAC*, Oak Ridge, Tennessee, USA.

## Verification Standards & Methodologies

### Verra VM0036 - Peatlands (Adapted for Permafrost)

**Description:** Verified Carbon Standard methodology for peatland carbon projects.

**Resources:**
- **Methodology:** VM0036 - Methodology for Rewetting Drained Temperate Peatlands
- **Website:** https://verra.org/methodologies/vm0036-methodology-for-rewetting-drained-temperate-peatlands/
- **Focus:** Peatland carbon stock changes and GHG emissions
- **Key Requirements:**
  - Stratification by peatland type and condition
  - Minimum 3 plots per stratum
  - 0-30 cm and 30-100 cm depth intervals
  - Conservative uncertainty treatment (95% CI lower bound)
  - 5-year monitoring frequency
  - CH₄ and N₂O flux monitoring (if significant)

**Adaptation for Permafrost:**
- Modify depth intervals for active layer variability
- Add permafrost-specific strata (polygon, palsa, thermokarst)
- Include active layer depth as key variable
- Separate active layer and permafrost carbon pools
- Address ground ice content corrections

**Relevance for This Workflow:**
- **Sampling design:** Minimum plot numbers and stratification
- **Depth standards:** Adapt 0-30, 30-100 cm to active layer constraints
- **Uncertainty:** Use conservative 95% CI approach
- **Verification:** 5-year monitoring cycle

**Citation:**
- Verra. (2020). VM0036 Methodology for Rewetting Drained Temperate Peatlands, Version 1.0. Verified Carbon Standard.

### IPCC Wetlands Supplement (2013)

**Description:** IPCC guidelines for greenhouse gas inventories in wetlands, including permafrost peatlands.

**Resources:**
- **Document:** 2013 Supplement to the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas Inventories: Wetlands

- **Key Sections:**
  - **Chapter 2:** Coastal wetlands (reference for methods)

**Tier Approach:**

- **Tier 2:** Regional emission factors
- **Tier 3:** Site-specific measurements (THIS WORKFLOW)

**Relevance for This Workflow:**
- **Carbon pools:** Definition of soil carbon pools
- **Depth intervals:** Standard depths for reporting
- **Emission factors:** CH₄ and N₂O from thermokarst (if applicable)
- **Uncertainty:** Tier 3 uncertainty propagation methods

**Citation:**
- IPCC. (2014). 2013 Supplement to the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas Inventories: Wetlands. IPCC, Switzerland.

## Data Integration Workflow

### Step 1: Pre-Field Planning

**Use these data sources:**
1. **NRCan Permafrost Maps** → Stratify by permafrost zone (continuous/discontinuous)
2. **IPA Ground Ice Map** → Identify high vs. low ice content areas
3. **GTN-P Borehole Sites** → Co-locate with existing monitoring if possible
4. **NTED** → Review existing carbon data for region
5. **Permafrost Pathways** → Check for nearby flux towers for validation

**Outputs:**
- Sampling design stratified by:
  - Permafrost zone
  - Wetland type (polygon, palsa, fen, thermokarst)
  - Ground ice content
  - Degradation status (intact vs. degrading)

---

### Step 2: Field Data Collection

**Protocols:**
- **Active layer depth:** CALM frost probe method
- **Soil cores:** Adapt VM0036 peatland protocol
  - Variable depths based on active layer (0-15, 15-30, 30-ALT cm, ALT-150 cm if possible)
- **Ground ice:** Gravimetric method (thaw frozen samples, measure water loss)
- **Site characterization:**
  - Vegetation type (sedge/moss/shrub %)
  - Microtopography (polygon type, palsa height)
  - Thermokarst features (pond proximity, subsidence)
  - GPS coordinates (WGS84)

**Data recording:**
- Use field data templates from CALM and PermafrostNet
- Record all PERMAFROST_MONITORING_VARS from config file

---

### Step 3: GEE Covariate Extraction

**Run:** `ARCTIC_PERMAFROST_COVARIATES.js`

**Required GEE datasets:**
- **ArcticDEM:** Elevation (if available for AOI)
- **ERA5-Land:** Climate data (temperature, snow)
- **Sentinel-2:** Summer vegetation indices
- **Sentinel-1:** SAR soil moisture
- **MODIS:** Snow cover duration
- **JRC Water:** Water occurrence (thermokarst ponds)

**Outputs:**
- Multi-band GeoTIFF with all covariates
- Individual key layers (active layer thickness, permafrost probability, temperature)

**Place in:** `data_raw/gee_covariates/`

---

### Step 4: R Workflow Processing

**Module sequence:**
1. **Module 01:** Data prep
   - Load field data
   - Apply QC thresholds (updated for Arctic)
   - Calculate ground ice-corrected bulk density
   - Flag permafrost vs. active layer samples

2. **Module 03:** Depth harmonization
   - **Active layer samples:** Use equal-area spline
   - **Permafrost samples:** Report separately (no interpolation through frozen layer)
   - **Variable ALT:** Harmonize to site-specific active layer depth

3. **Module 05:** Random Forest spatial prediction
   - Use Arctic-specific covariates
   - Train separate models for:
     - Active layer carbon
     - Permafrost carbon (if sampled)
   - Include active layer depth as critical predictor

4. **Module 07:** MMRV Reporting
   - Report active layer vs. permafrost carbon separately
   - Include climate vulnerability metrics
   - Compare to CMIP6 projections
   - Cite relevant Canadian data sources

---

### Step 5: Verification & Validation

**Independent validation:**
1. **NTED comparison:** Compare site-level carbon stocks to NTED literature values
2. **CanSIS profiles:** Validate bulk density and depth distribution
3. **GTN-P active layer:** Compare measured ALT to GTN-P records (if co-located)
4. **CALM trends:** Compare inter-annual ALT variability to CALM network trends

**Standards compliance:**
- **VM0036 (adapted):** Check minimum cores per stratum, precision targets
- **IPCC Tier 3:** Ensure site-specific measurements meet Tier 3 requirements
- **CALM protocols:** Verify active layer measurement methodology

## Data Citation Best Practices

When using these data sources in publications or reports:

1. **Primary data sources:** Cite original data providers (NRCan, GTN-P, etc.)
2. **Derived products:** Acknowledge processing steps and algorithms
3. **Open data:** Include DOIs where available
4. **Monitoring networks:** Acknowledge CALM, GTN-P, PermafrostNet coordinators
5. **Government data:** Cite Natural Resources Canada and provincial agencies

**Example citation block for reports:**

> This analysis utilized permafrost distribution data from Natural Resources Canada (NRCan, 2024), active layer thickness measurements from the Circumpolar Active Layer Monitoring (CALM) Network (Brown et al., 2000), and soil carbon literature values from the Northern Terrestrial Ecosystem Database (Bockheim et al., 2015). Spatial covariates were derived from Sentinel-2 and ERA5-Land via Google Earth Engine. Field protocols were adapted from CALM (frost probing) and Verra VM0036 (soil coring and carbon analysis).
